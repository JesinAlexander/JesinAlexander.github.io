MUSIC AND TODDLERS

Toddlers enjoy moving and dancing to music. The secret to toddler music is song repetition, which help them to understand word and increase their memorising power.Music captivates the attention of young children. Babies are able to distinguish subtleties and variations in pitch and rhythm in songs in other languages that even their parents don't notice because of this curiosity.Infants have remarkably good listening abilities. Infants and adults both have similar ways of understanding music. They are able to distinguish between songs with missing or discordant notes as well as the same song played at a different pitch. Babies recall and prefer nice sound to unfavourable music, just like adults do.Music is extremely well-remembered by infants. Infants may recognise familiar melodies and recall songs they have heard after being exposed to them for 1-2 weeks. Though interested and stimulated by something new, they prefer new music over the familiar.
