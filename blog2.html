WEANING YOUR BABY

The best method of weaning is to gradually introduce new foods and intimacy. Ideally, nursing sessions should become shorter and shorter over the course of several weeks or even months. In this way, before you quit breastfeeding entirely, you and your kid will have enough time to find alternative ways to keep your bond strong, to express and accept comfort or tenderness, and to make sure they're getting enough nutrients. Additionally, you will reduce your own physical challenges like breast engorgement and prevent the arguments and resistance that often result from more sudden weaning. One of the best ways to start a slow weaning process with a child who is one or older is to just wait to give them a feeding and see if they ask for it.
