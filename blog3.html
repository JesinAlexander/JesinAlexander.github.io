FEVER IN BABY

The age of your child and the thermometer reading will determine how you should address a fever as a parent. When children's energy levels are low and their fevers are high (above 38.8°C / 102°F), you know something is wrong. Children between the ages of 2 and 11 tend to run higher fevers than adults do. Here are some tips to help lower their fever even when they might be fighting an infection: Icon of a rubber ducky Bring warm water to a bath. However, if the water makes your kids shiver, take them out of the bath because chills might cause their temperature to rise. Icon of a water bottle Give plenty of water for drinking. Dehydration and fever are often related. But advise children to avoid soda because it contains caffeine.
